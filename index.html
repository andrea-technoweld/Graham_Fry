The Approach

Thorough, independent, and focused on outcomes.

Many organisations engage welding consultants only after a problem has already escalated. Graham works with clients before, during, and after critical project phases.

His approach is methodical and uncompromising. Every engagement begins with a thorough review of the goal, "clearly defining of the problem", because the risks or issues that matter are rarely the obvious ones.

Whether the work involves setting up a new facility, getting a welding production line running properly, reducing work, development of Welding Procedure Specifications from ground up, reviewing quality management frameworks against ISO 3834, or working through a compliance challenge before it becomes a project stoppage, the methodology applied is the same: practical application of deep technical knowledge.

Graham has worked with clients who have been advised that a project cannot proceed or cannot be saved. In the majority of cases, a viable path forward exists. Finding it requires a thorough command of the trade first principles, existing / new practices, having the "know how", and the persistence to work the problem through to resolution.

Areas of Expertise

Technical Services

Core services, each aimed at the welding problems that are painful for clients, difficult to navigate, reduce competitiveness, risk rework or rejection — all add to costs that quietly erode your margin, delay projects, or end up in dispute.

Process Optimisation

Most welding cost lives in the process, not the price of wire. Graham reviews how work actually flows through your shop and finds where heat input, over-welding, handling, and unnecessary PWHT are quietly draining margin, then sets a leaner process that holds quality.

Design For Manufacture

The cost of a weld is decided at the drawing board. Graham reviews joint design, access, and detailing before fabrication starts, removing the over-specified joints, awkward positions, and unnecessary processes that drive labour, consumables, and rework on the floor.

Welding Issue Resolution

When a weld keeps failing and nobody can say why, production stops and costs climb. Graham works the problem on the floor, isolates the real cause across technique, parameters, material, or preparation, and gets you back to producing sound welds quickly.

Welding Management System Development

A quality system that reflects what really happens on the floor, not a folder of templates for the auditor. Graham develops practical welding management systems to ISO 3834 that control defects systemically and open the door to work you are currently filtered out of.

Vendor Assessment and Development

Accepting a fabricator on trust is how rework and rejected product end up on your project. Graham assesses supplier capability against AS/NZS 5131 and ISO 3834, verifies they can deliver what they have promised, and works with them to close the gaps.

Independent Technical Review

A second set of expert eyes before you commit. Graham provides third-party review of welding decisions, fabrication packages, and inspection outcomes, including where you have been told something cannot be done. More often than not, a viable and cheaper path exists.

Customised Training / Mentoring

Generic courses do not fix your problems. Graham builds training and mentoring around your work, your standards, and your team, from welders through to supervisors and engineers, so capability is lifted where it actually reduces your rejection rate and reliance on outside help.

Failure Analysis

A weld failure is a systems failure, not just a broken joint. Graham investigates failures across industries and materials, goes past the immediate cause to the root factors, and gives you the findings needed to prevent recurrence and support any claim that follows.

Expert Witness

Independent welding engineering evidence when a job has gone to dispute, insurance claim, or litigation. Graham provides clear written and oral expert evidence, grounded in the standards and 30 years of practice, that holds up under scrutiny from the other side.

Why Graham Fry

Expertise that has been tested on the most demanding projects.

Graham Fry has worked as a welding engineering consultant since the mid-1990s, across pressure equipment, structural steel, defence, mining, and oil and gas projects in Australia and internationally. Over that period, he has taken on the technical problems other welding consultants have declined: unusual material combinations, competing standard requirements, failed welder and procedure qualifications in remote or time-critical environments, and complex weld failure investigations with significant commercial and legal implications.

One thing separates Graham from most welding consultants: he can put the helmet on and show you. When a procedure is failing or a welder is struggling with a difficult joint, Graham doesn't just diagnose the problem from a report. He demonstrates the fix under the hood, in front of you or your tradespeople, and proves it can be done. Talk is cheap in this industry. Demonstration isn't.

What distinguishes his work is not only the depth of technical knowledge across Australian and international welding standards, but the commitment he makes to each engagement. Graham does not close an engagement when the answer becomes difficult. He has remained on projects well beyond the scope of an initial brief because the client needed a genuine resolution, not an interim recommendation.

For organisations operating in industries where the consequences of welding failures are serious, structurally, financially, or reputationally, that standard of commitment is not a preference. It is a requirement.

Graham Fry has built his reputation on solving the problems that others have assessed as unsolvable. More often than not, the limiting factor is not the standard — it is the depth of the investigation.
