About Graham

Graham Fry

Welding Engineering Consultant | International Welding Engineer (IWE)

Most consultants in welding and fabrication fall into one of two camps. There's the tradespeople who've spent decades on the tools but never formalised what they know. Then there's the engineers who can quote every clause of ISO 3834 but haven't struck an arc in years.

Graham did it the long way. Apprenticeship first, National Tradesperson of the Year, then the full IIW ladder from Welding Practitioner through to International Welding Engineer. Thirty years on, he still puts the helmet on, because telling a fabricator what's possible means nothing unless you can show them.

Graham's career started where it matters most: on the shop floor. In 1988, he began his apprenticeship at Pipetech Pty Ltd, then the country's leading pressure equipment manufacturer, fabricating vessels and associated structures for the oil, gas, chemical and food industries. For ten years he welded, fabricated, problem-solved and learned the hard way what works and what doesn't when metal meets heat. That foundation isn't something you can get from a textbook, and it's something he carries into every job he does today.

But Graham didn't stop at the trade. He went the other way as well, chasing formal qualifications with the same intensity he brought to the workshop. He holds the European/International Welding Engineer designation, an Incorporated Engineer credential, Diplomas in both Welding Engineering and Welding Technology, a Bachelor of Education (Adult and Vocational), and an Advanced Diploma of Engineering in Manufacturing. He earned a place in the Golden Key Society for academic honours along the way.

His inspection credentials cover the UK, the US, the EU/ISO framework, and Australia/New Zealand: CSWIP 3.1, 3.2.1 and 3.2.2, AWS CWI, IIW International Welding Inspector (Comprehensive & Standard), Welding Supervisor certifications to AS 1796 and AS 2214 and NACE Level 3 Senior Coating Inspector.

In 1995, after finishing his apprenticeship, he was awarded the WTIA/Linde National Tradesperson of the Year scholarship.

And he never put the helmet down. Graham still welds regularly during training, on everything from basic gas metal arc welding through to exotic materials, and serves as the technical lead for many organisations.

The qualifications opened doors. The hood time is why clients keep him around.

Why This Combination Matters

He's been on both sides of the table.

When Graham walks into a fabrication shop, he doesn't just see what's wrong on an audit checklist. He sees what's actually happening. He can watch a welder set up a joint and know, from thirty years of doing it himself, whether the technique, the parameters, or the preparation will cause problems before a single weld test gets run. That's not a skill you develop in a classroom, and it's not one most welding consultants can offer.

When he sits down with engineers, asset owners or project managers to work through welding procedure development, welder qualification, ISO 3834 quality management systems, or compliance to AS/NZS 1554, AS 3992 and ASME codes, he speaks their language too. He can translate between the shop floor and the engineering office because he's lived in both worlds, as a tradesperson, a welding inspector, and an International Welding Engineer.

This is what separates Graham from most welding and fabrication consultants. A tradesperson might spot the problem but struggle to put it in terms that satisfy an engineer or an auditor. An engineer might write the perfect weld procedure on paper but miss how it actually gets executed in a workshop running three shifts. Graham bridges that gap because he doesn't have to guess at either side. He's been there.

30 Years of Building Businesses

Founded in 1996. Still at it.

Graham has founded multiple companies across the welding and fabrication industry, the cornerstone being the national welding consultancy he established in 1996. Thirty years on, that business carries a heritage few in Australia can match: welding management consulting, inspection, procedure development, training, and quality system implementation delivered to clients across Australia and internationally.

The work spans pressure vessels, high-pressure piping, structural steel, and everything in between, across oil and gas, mining, defence, chemical, infrastructure and food processing sectors. There's very little in the welding and fabrication space the business hasn't encountered, and very little Graham hasn't personally worked on.

He's also spent over a decade as a lecturer at TAFE SA, teaching welding supervision, inspection, senior fabrication inspection and welder certification. Training isn't a sideline for Graham. He's seen first-hand what happens when tradespeople don't get the right support and education, and he's built businesses to fix exactly that.

Industry Leadership

Graham doesn't just work in the welding industry. He's helped shape it.

Australian Welding Institute

Founding Director of AWI in 2010. Served on the Federal Board of Directors for over a decade, including roles as Technical Director, Director of Marketing, and Chair of the Education Certification Committee.

Standards Development

Sat on the committees responsible for developing AS 1554 and AS 3992 — the standards that Australian fabricators and welders work to every day. This is standards authorship, not just standards compliance.

International Engagement

Keynote speaker at Welding Technology Asia 2012. Attended IIW Annual Assemblies in Singapore, Slovakia, Indonesia, Melbourne and beyond. Multiple visits to the Essen Welding Trade Show in Shanghai and Germany.

WTIA Board Leadership

Held senior positions on the WTIA Federal Board, including Chair of Federal Divisional Board and Chair of State Division. Represented Australian industry at the International Institute of Welding's national committee level.

Education and Training

More than a decade lecturing at TAFE SA across welding supervision, inspection, senior fabrication inspection and welder certification. Developed the AWI Welding Supervision Certification Scheme used in Australia today, and set up the first International Welding Specialist (IWS) correspondence course in the country.

National Recognition

Awarded the WTIA/Linde National Tradesperson of the Year scholarship in 1995 — recognition that standing out as exceptional in the welding industry starts with doing the work properly at every level.
