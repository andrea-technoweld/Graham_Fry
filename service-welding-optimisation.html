Your Welding Process Is Probably Costing You More Than It Should

Fabrication businesses rarely struggle because they lack welders. More often, productivity issues originate from welding processes that have never been systematically optimised — procedures developed years ago and left unchanged as equipment, consumables, and production demands have moved on.

What it actually means

More Than Just Welding Faster

Welding optimisation is the systematic improvement of welding operations to maximise productivity, quality, and process control across the entire fabrication process. It's not about pushing welders harder — it's about engineering the system around them properly.

Because welding involves complex interactions between heat input, metallurgy, and mechanical performance, improvements must be implemented carefully to maintain compliance with relevant standards and design requirements. The goal is procedures that are both technically robust and efficient for production use.

In many workshops, relatively small changes to welding procedures, joint design, or parameter control can significantly increase deposition efficiency and reduce welding cycle time — without touching the workforce at all.

Common Problems in Fabrication Workshops

In many workshops, businesses initially attribute productivity problems to labour shortages or workload pressure. Detailed analysis often reveals the primary issue lies in process inefficiency.

⚙️

Manual welding where automation would help

Semi-automatic or mechanised methods would significantly increase deposition rate, but manual processes remain in place from habit.

📄

Procedures written for qualification, not production

WPS documents developed primarily to pass a test, with conservative parameters that don't reflect what's achievable in production.

📏

Oversized welds throughout the structure

Fillet welds larger than the drawing requires. Every extra millimetre of weld leg is wasted time, consumables, and heat input.

🔧

High repair and rework rates

Recurring weld defects and repairs that consume significant time but whose root cause has never been systematically investigated.

🔋

Underutilised modern equipment

Power sources with advanced waveform and pulsed modes sitting on conservative settings because nobody has reviewed their configuration.

⏱

Welding as a production bottleneck

Other trades waiting on welding to complete. Project schedules driven by welding throughput rather than total fabrication capacity.

A structured engineering methodology

The Optimisation Approach

Rather than focusing only on the welding arc, the approach considers the entire fabrication system. Welding productivity is strongly influenced by joint design, fit-up quality, consumable selection, and production workflow. By analysing these elements together, it becomes possible to identify opportunities that are often overlooked in day-to-day workshop operations.

Is this the right time?

When to Consider Welding Optimisation Consulting

Many businesses seek support when persistent production or quality issues emerge. But optimisation also provides significant value during periods of growth or technology transition — before inefficiencies become embedded in production systems.

Early optimisation is always less expensive than retrofitting fixes to an established but poorly performing process.
